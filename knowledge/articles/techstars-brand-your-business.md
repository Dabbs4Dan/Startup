# Brand Your Business

## Core thesis
Branding builds emotional connections that increase perceived value. For startups, mastering the brand identity discipline early helps attract customers, investors, and accelerate growth — but **it takes 28 repetitions** for a customer to remember your brand, so consistency and patience matter more than cleverness.

## Frameworks

### Brand Identity Map
- **What:** Tool for developing consistent messaging about your company/product so internal and external comms align.
- **Use:** Single source of truth for what the company stands for.

### Positioning Statement
- **What:** "Your most repeated message that quickly describes what you do and the benefits to your target audience."
- **Components:** Brand name + category descriptors paired together; features paired with **emotionally resonant imagery**; tangible benefits paired with feeling.
- **Purpose:** Build brand awareness; entice the audience to learn more.

## Tactics
- Identify your **top 3–5 purchase features/benefits** versus competitors, not all of them.
- Discover what **emotions** customers feel when interacting with your product (not what they think — what they feel).
- Pair every feature with imagery that evokes the right emotion.
- Craft one positioning statement and use it everywhere — consistency is the multiplier.
- Repeat, repeat, repeat — the **28-times threshold** is real.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **It takes 28 repetitions** for a customer to remember your brand. Most founders give up at 3–5 because they've heard themselves say it 100 times.

## Examples
- **MakerBot** — Chuck Pettis (cited speaker) was VP of Marketing; the company achieved a $400M exit.
