# Category Design — Understand + Create New Markets

> Combines two related Techstars Toolkit pages: [Understand Category Design](https://toolkit.techstars.com/understand-category-design) and [Create New Markets With Category Design](https://toolkit.techstars.com/create-new-markets-category-design).

## Core thesis
Don't compete inside an existing category — *design a new one*. Category Design is **company strategy, not marketing** — it works by identifying problems no incumbent owns, naming the new space, and becoming the "Category King" of it. Differentiation > incremental improvement.

## Frameworks

### Category Lifecycle Model
- **What:** Three phases of category maturity that determine investment opportunity and strategic posture.
- **Use:** Understand where your space sits to know whether to dominate, disrupt, or wait.

### Adjacent Possible
- **What:** Balance technological advancement with societal adoption readiness.
- **Use:** Prevents building products *too far ahead* of market adoption — name a category the world is ready for.

### Category Design Strategy (7 components)
1. **Focus on the Problem** — not the solution.
2. **Create a Category Name** — utilitarian, simple, easy to understand.
3. **Manage Your Suit Size** — design the category name for future growth, not just today.
4. **Start New Conversations** — use "Lightning Strikes" to shift mindsets toward the new category.
5. **Set Process and Timeline** — category development is multi-year, not multi-quarter.
6. **Build Category Blueprint** — visual roadmap of how you solve the customer problem.
7. **Develop Point of View** — emotionally resonant narrative, not just logical pitch.

### Lightning Strikes
- **What:** Mobilization tactics to shift attention away from the existing mental model toward your new category.
- **Use:** Direct market attention rapidly when launching the category.

### Category Blueprint
- **What:** Visual document showing how you solve the customer problem.
- **Why:** "Showing, not just telling" — communicates the category vision faster than prose.

## Tactics
- Look for **zero-billion-dollar markets** — spaces no one has named or claimed yet — rather than fighting Category Kings in existing markets.
- Apply category design **early-stage**, not as a late-stage rebrand. Early-stage entrepreneurs gain disproportionate advantage against larger incumbents who are locked into existing category mental models.
- Pick a category name **simple enough to be repeated and explained quickly** — complex names kill market education.
- Build the emotional/instinct-appealing narrative first — the logical case follows.
- Plan for a **multi-year horizon** — category creation isn't a sprint.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"Think different, not just better"** — succeed by differentiation, not incremental improvement on the same axis.
- **Category Design is *not* marketing or branding** — it's foundational company strategy. If you treat it as a comms exercise, you'll lose the strategic leverage.
- **Avoid competing directly with Category Kings.** The right move is to redefine the category, not battle for share of the existing one.

## Examples
- **iPad** — created an entire product category by defining a problem users didn't realize they had.
- **Airbnb** — Category King of the shared economy lodging space.
- **Replicon** — case study of category design used to "redesign marketing" and compete differently rather than fight in crowded enterprise software.
