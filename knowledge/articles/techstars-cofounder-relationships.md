# Navigate Cofounder Relationships

## Core thesis
**~65% of startup failures come from cofounder breakdown** — not market fit, not capital. Strong communication structures and explicit written agreements prevent the splits that kill companies.

## Frameworks

### Seven Steps to Founder Communication Success
1. **Avoid Cofounder Relationship Fails** — recognize early warning patterns.
2. **Get Your House in Order** — agreements, equity, decision rights *in writing*.
3. **Understand Your Cofounder** — strengths, weaknesses, goals, needs.
4. **Set Clear Roles** — who decides what; how disagreements escalate.
5. **Discuss the Elephant in the Room** — name the avoided topic before it becomes existential.
6. **Build Trust With Your Cofounder** — sustained, not declared.
7. **Cofounder Conversation Exercise** — structured dialogue tool (worksheet).

## Tactics
- Develop a **healthy rhythm of communication** — weekly explicit check-ins, not just ambient overlap.
- Identify your cofounder's strengths, weaknesses, goals, and needs *deliberately*.
- Define **who does what and how decisions get made** before you need to make a hard one.
- Address concerns **early and respectfully** before they escalate to existential.
- **Get cofounder and investor agreements in writing before growth accelerates** — when stakes are low is the time to formalize.
- Use the cofounder conversation worksheet to "craft conversation starters" and strengthen alignment.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **65% of startup breakups come from people issues, not market or capital.** This reframes failure-mode analysis: the most common killer is interpersonal, not strategic.
- **Get formal *before* you need to.** Founders procrastinate on agreements because trust is high — that's exactly when to write them down, because writing them down later is a fight.
