# Build a Diverse Workforce

## Core thesis
Diversity is a **journey, not a checkbox**, and it's framed as **business strategy, not obligation** — diverse companies show 90% higher retention. Concrete actions are available to founders of any size starting today; the leverage isn't ideology, it's the structural changes that prevent unconscious bias from running the hiring funnel.

## Frameworks

### G.I.V.E. (Kapor Capital's Founders' Commitment)
Four pillars:
- **G — Goals:** Assign a single diversity leader (not a committee). Collect demographic data on employees and customers.
- **I — Invest:** Make sure "audio matches video" — fair compensation, updated codes of conduct, inclusive mission statements.
- **V — Volunteer:** Build diverse networks via mentoring, community partnerships, conferences. Avoid stereotyping communities.
- **E — Educate:** Recognize unconscious biases through implicit bias testing. Address coded language like "fit."

### 4-2-50% Rule (recruitment counter-bias)
- With **4 candidates (1 woman, 3 men)**, the female-hire odds are near zero.
- With **2 women and 2 men balanced**, odds become ~50%.
- The structural fix removes the advantage simply by changing the pool composition. **No interview-process improvement required for the math to flip.**

## Tactics
- Assign **one person** explicit responsibility for diversity work — diffused ownership equals no ownership.
- Collect data on employee and customer demographics to identify blind spots (you can't fix what you don't measure).
- **Audit compensation across all demographics** — this is the truest signal of whether the policy is real.
- Take the **Harvard Implicit Bias Test** to identify your own biases.
- Attend diversity-focused conferences (gender, race, entrepreneurship).
- Ensure **balanced candidate pools** — minimum 2 representatives per demographic.
- Evaluate marketing messaging for representation.
- Partner with university Chief Diversity Officers for recruitment.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"Audio should match video."** What you say must align with what you actually do. **Compensation is the truest measure** — anything else is positioning.
- **Diverse companies show 90% higher retention** — diversity is business strategy, not obligation. The economic case is stronger than the moral one for most audiences, even if both apply.
- **Unconscious bias affects everyone, including diversity professionals.** The presenter discovered bias against their *own* race. Self-flattery about being unbiased is the bias.
- **"Fit" is a code word** masking unconscious bias decisions, not a legitimate hiring criterion. Strike the word.
- **Avoid the charity-model of volunteering.** Building diverse networks requires leaving assumptions about communities at the door.

## Examples
- **Hypothetical:** A company with six male engineers and no women discovers women hate their product while men love it — demographic imbalance explains the product feedback gap. Diversity is a *product* lever, not just a hiring lever.
- **Anecdote:** CEO remembered a candidate years after seeing her present at a competing hospital — hired her years later when a position opened. Long-arc relationship building beats reactive recruiting.
- **Population data:** By 2050, India, China, and Nigeria lead population growth. The future-customer demographic is not the past-founder demographic.
