# Build Your Investor Pipeline

## Core thesis
Fundraising is a **slog** that requires preparation across three phases — target, pitch, close. The single biggest leverage is having your "Mise En Place" (all materials prepared and organized) *before* you contact a single investor.

## Frameworks

### Three Phases of Fundraising
1. **Who to target** — identify investors that fit
2. **The pitch** — articulate what you do
3. **The close** — secure commitments

### Mise En Place (the prep checklist)
Prepare all of these *before* approaching anyone:
- Investor pipeline spreadsheet
- Process Starter email
- Investor CRM
- Forwardable email
- One-pager
- Emailable deck
- Meeting deck
- Financial model
- Cap table
- Data room

### Investor Pipeline Spreadsheet (15 columns)
Fund name · location · ranking · tranche · fund size · ticket size · domain · investor name/title · contact info · type of interest · reasons for fit · introduction helper · intro status · notes.

### CRM Status Categories
**New → Advancing → Soft Circled → Committed → Legal → Wired** · plus **Passed**.

### Forwardable Email (the one trick)
- **Brief and scannable**
- **Investor-specific relevance** (named, not generic)
- **Clear reason for outreach**
- **No editing required from forwarder** — they should be able to send unchanged from their phone

## Tactics
- Invest in mentor + founder relationships **before** fundraising begins, especially among those who share your background or industry focus.
- Identify key milestones to **time fundraising optimally** — raise from a position of momentum, not desperation.
- Send a **Process Starter email** to mentors stating your timeline, target investor types, and specific names.
- Build the pipeline spreadsheet and **share with mentors for feedback** — this is also a soft way to surface intros.
- Transfer to CRM once you have actual investor traction.
- Use **forwardable emails** that contacts can pass along unchanged from mobile.
- **Proactively track "passed" investors** — they may re-engage as momentum builds.
- Evaluate preemptive offers by assessing investor fit, timing, and whether you still need to complete the round.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"It's easier to have the market push up the amount and valuation, instead of you doing the pushing."** Don't try to set the price — let competitive demand do it.
- **The fundraising process is "a slog."** Mentally prepare that it will *NEVER* be easy, regardless of success stories. The romantic version is survivor narrative; the lived version is grind.
- **Engage with early investor interest even when not raising.** Building reputation and relationships off-cycle pays compounding returns when you do raise.

## Examples
- **Sample Process Starter Email:** Company in Techstars accelerator · $20K MRR · 3X revenue growth post-Techstars · 5 new paid pilots · targeting angels and seed funds focused on revenue-generating SaaS.
- **Sample Forwardable Email:** Company X seeking introduction to "Angel Angelino," recent investor in comparable companies "Blah" and "YaDaYa."
