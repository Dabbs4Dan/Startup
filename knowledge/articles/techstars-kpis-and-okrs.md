# KPIs + OKRs — Measuring Progress That's Actually Progress

> Combines three closely-related Techstars Toolkit pages: [Grow Your Business With KPIs](https://toolkit.techstars.com/grow-your-business), [Make Progress With Goals](https://toolkit.techstars.com/make-progress), [Check Your Progress](https://toolkit.techstars.com/check-your-progress).

## Core thesis
Founders confuse motion with progress. **Activity and outputs aren't outcomes.** The remedy is a tight discipline: pick a small set of KPIs that matter, frame work as Objectives + Key Results (OKRs), and review weekly to surface variance between expected and actual.

## Frameworks

### KPI Framework
- **Components:**
  - **Growth KPIs** — expansion metrics
  - **Value KPIs** — customer-value-delivered metrics
  - **Regular review cadence** — weekly check-ins
  - **Customer experience mapping** to transaction moments

### Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)
- **Two-part goal-setting:**
  - **Objectives** — describe what you want to achieve and *why*
  - **Key Results** — measurable outcomes that indicate whether progress is occurring
- **Purpose:** Shift focus from task completion to outcome measurement.

### Workflow Management for Progress
- Prioritize work delivering **immediate customer value**.
- Balance current tasks with forward-looking objectives.
- Maintain alignment between daily work and OKRs.
- Establish consistent workflow cadence; finish work before starting new.
- Manage capacity by limiting commitments — understand the consequences of over-committing.

## Tactics
- **Identify 2–3 measurable KPIs that matter to your business *today*.** Not the dashboard you'd want at scale — the few that move now.
- Conduct **weekly check-ins** with team and investors to review goals, KPIs, and surface issues.
- Map the **customer experience leading up to transaction moments** — that journey is where most KPIs live.
- When data is unavailable, use "the easiest and fastest measurements" as evidence.
- **Reset weekly goals based on the gap between expected and actual results** — this learning loop is the engine.
- Ruthlessly prioritize. Say "no" to excess commitments.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"One can easily mistake motion for progress, when in fact they are not the same thing."** Many startups treat being busy as progress — it isn't.
- **Entrepreneurship is a blend of art and science.** KPIs create numerical baselines for gut responses, they don't replace intuition. In low-data moments, trust judgment but use metrics as validation.
- **Activities and outputs aren't outcomes.** This sounds obvious; founders violate it daily by celebrating tickets-closed instead of needles-moved.
