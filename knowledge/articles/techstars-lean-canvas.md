# Build Your Lean Canvas

## Core thesis
The Lean Canvas is a 9-component planning tool for moving "from your best guess with the best evidence" by systematically pressure-testing customers, problems, solutions, and the business model on a single page — replacing intuition-driven planning with validated business thinking.

## Frameworks

### The Lean Canvas
- **What:** A single-page business planning canvas with 9 components.
- **Components:** Customer segments · their problems · proposed solution · unique value proposition · channels · key metrics · cost structure · revenue streams · unfair advantage.
- **Process note:** "The order in which you complete the canvas matters a lot." Start with **customer understanding** before building solution and revenue evidence.

## Tactics
- Build the canvas collaboratively with your team using pen and paper.
- Identify specific customer segments and their shared characteristics.
- Document how customers describe their *own* problems — use their language, not yours.
- Define the "unfair advantage" — what uniquely positions your team that competitors can't replicate.
- Map distribution channels to actual target customers.
- Iterate the canvas based on evidence, not refinement of the original idea.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"Start beating the crappiness out of your ideas"** through structured canvas analysis rather than continued refinement of the concept in your head.
- **The canvas isn't the work — filling it with evidence is.** Treat boxes you can't fill confidently as research targets, not gaps to paper over.
