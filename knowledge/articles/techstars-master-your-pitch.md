# Master Your Pitch

## Core thesis
A pitch is your moment to **entertain while demonstrating low-risk, high-return investment potential**. Investors don't evaluate whether your product is innovative — they evaluate whether you'll make *them* money and whether you can win and de-risk their bet. Delivery quality matters as much as content quality.

## Frameworks

### The 5-minute Pitch Structure
- **Intro:** ~1 minute
- **Demo:** ~2–3 minutes
- **Business section ("Biz Stuff"):** ~1–1.5 minutes
- **Landing:** ~30 seconds

### Introduction Components (30–60 seconds)
1. Introduce yourself and brand
2. Establish credibility
3. Illuminate the problem

**Hooks that work:** interesting fact · personal story · contrasting conventional wisdom · humor · creating fear.

### Business Section Components
- Market size · business model and pricing pathway · differentiation and competitive positioning · team · timeline showing growth/accomplishments · traction/execution data.

### Landing Strategy
- Restate top features and benefits
- Specific call-to-action
- **Request something concrete** — not "any thoughts?" — name what you want.

## Tactics
- Spend **75% of preparation time on the introduction** — without it, the rest is moot.
- Make your pitch **"more interesting than their cell phone"** — you're competing against distraction, not other pitches.
- Use visuals with minimal text. **Show, don't tell** — demos beat verbal descriptions.
- Remove bullet points from slides — bullets force the audience to read instead of listen.
- Vary delivery speed and tone. Use **strategic silence** for emphasis.
- Avoid generic descriptors. Explain *how* you do what you do, not *what* you are.
- Practice "as though your company is on the line."
- Prepare answers to your **top 5 anticipated questions**.
- If you don't know an answer, **admit it honestly and offer follow-up** — never bluff.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"I'd rather watch D-level content with A-level delivery than A-level content with D-level delivery."** Delivery dominates content perception.
- **Investors don't actually care about competition** — they care whether *you* can win and de-risk their investment. Stop spending pitch time on competitor matrices.
- **People will read slides OR listen — never both.** Simple visuals trump complex data on stage.
- **Silence re-engages distracted investors.** A pause forces them to look up from their phones.

## Examples (all Techstars portfolio)
- **Keen.io** (Boulder) — used storytelling and metaphor to explain a technical product; buried market opportunity in narrative.
- **ScriptPad** (Boulder) — used "twist the knife" emotional technique showing prescription safety problems repeatedly.
- **Jukely** (NYC) — live music subscription; visual demo illustrated business model + acquisition + competitive advantage simultaneously.
- **SendGrid** (IPO) — communicated market size creatively despite an unprecedented business model.
- **Dash** — founder's framing of differentiation and why product beats competition.
- **Next Big Sound** (acquired by Pandora) — creative team presentation approach.
