# Position Your Product

## Core thesis
A pitch is a **continuous business-building process**, not a one-time event. Effective positioning connects emotionally to customer pain *before* describing what your company does. People buy on emotion and justify with logic — own the emotion first.

## Frameworks

### Positioning Framework (5 components)
1. **Target audience** — specifically who you serve (not "small businesses" — name the role + context)
2. **Pain points** — the customer problems you address (in their words)
3. **Value proposition** — what you offer that eases their lives
4. **Competitors** — anything distracting customers from your solution (this includes time, alternatives, phones, social activities — *not just rival companies*)
5. **Differentiator** — what makes you distinctly valuable

**Worked example (Pivot Desk, Techstars 2012, acquired by SquareFoot):**
- Target: commercial real estate brokers and entrepreneurs
- Pain: can't find flexible leasing solutions
- Differentiator: non-traditional matching marketplace

## Tactics
- Be ready to launch your pitch the **moment** someone asks "what do you do?" — make it ingrained through repetition.
- Maintain a **single core pitch** — vary opening by audience, but keep the messaging consistent.
- Watch audience reactions in the wild: **confusion vs. requests for more info** = data on whether the pitch resonates.
- Expect to iterate **80+ versions** before reaching effective.
- Remove extra information ruthlessly. **Simplicity is hard work**, not the easy default.
- Deliver with equal energy to each new listener regardless of fatigue — flat delivery kills good content.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **"Nobody buys because of logic. They buy because of emotion and justify with logic."** Positioning that leads with features is positioning that's losing.
- **Competitors aren't only rival companies.** They're anything that takes attention away from your solution — time itself is a competitor.
- **Poor pitch reactions indicate business problems, not just communication failures:** *"if no one is reacting, then you haven't nailed the business."* The pitch *is* a diagnostic.
- **Simplicity is complex.** Stripping a pitch to its essence requires extensive iteration — it's not the natural starting point.

## Examples
- **Pivot Desk** — opened a real estate broker event pitch with **"I f***ing hate real estate"** to connect emotionally before explaining the solution. Funny, jarring, on-brand for the audience's actual pain.
