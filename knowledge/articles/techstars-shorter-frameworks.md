# Techstars Toolkit — Shorter Frameworks

> Combined extract of 5 Techstars Toolkit pages where the page itself is too thin to merit a standalone note (most depth lives in linked videos, not the page text). Captured here so the corpus has the framework names and key claims without padding the index with stubs.

---

## 1. Elevator Pitch — 30-second framework

**URL:** https://toolkit.techstars.com/give-your-elevator-pitch · **Speaker:** Nicole Glaros

**Components:**
1. The customers you serve
2. The problem they have
3. The solution your product provides

**Constraint:** under 30 seconds.

**Purpose:** Capture investor or prospective customer attention.

**Note:** No examples, no template, no counterintuitive content on the page itself — content is in linked videos.

---

## 2. Get More Done — Productivity System (David Brown, Techstars cofounder)

**URL:** https://toolkit.techstars.com/get-more-done

**Seven components:**
1. Stay Organized
2. Tame Your Inbox
3. Manage Your Tasks
4. Keep a Clear Mind With Tasks at Center
5. Keep a Clear Mind With End of Day Ritual
6. Enable Team Productivity
7. Bring On Help

**One claim worth keeping:** *"Would you spend a minute to save an hour? Probably. Yet many people don't."* Acknowledges the paradox: people recognize the value of organizational systems but fail to invest in building them.

## 3. 21 Ways to Blow Up Your Company (Jason Mendelson)

**URL:** https://toolkit.techstars.com/blow-up-your-company

**Six categorical failure modes:**
1. **Mess Up Company Basics** — lacking foundational practices
2. **Ignore Your Customers** — neglecting customer needs and competitive landscape
3. **Hire Bad Employees** — poor performance or cultural misalignment
4. **Fundraise Without Intent** — standardized rather than tailored approaches
5. **Follow Bad Advice** — incomplete paperwork, poor counsel
6. **Lack Leadership Skills** — inadequate personal development for the demands of 24/7 entrepreneurship

**Tactics named on page:**
- Eye on customer + competition + public image
- Tailored approach to each prospective investor
- Complete key paperwork promptly
- Develop personal leadership capabilities to match business growth
- Attract employees aligned with company culture

**Note:** No specific failure stories or numbered "21 ways" enumerated on the page — that's in the videos.

## 4. The W3 Framework — Who, What, Why (Amos Schwartzfarb)

**URL:** https://toolkit.techstars.com/w3-who-what-why-framework

**What:** Tool for moving from customer development to repeatable sales by answering three questions.

**The three questions:** Who · What · Why — but the actual content of those questions is in the videos and the worksheet, not the page.

**Attribution:** Amos Schwartzfarb (former Managing Director, Techstars Austin); books **"Sell More Faster"** and **"Levers."**

**Note:** The page is essentially a landing page; the framework details aren't on the public page itself.

## 5. Entrepreneurship & Mental Health (Documentary series)

**URL:** https://toolkit.techstars.com/entrepreneurship-mental-health

**Format:** Four-part Techstars documentary series, not a framework page.

**Featured stories:**
- **Brad Feld** (Techstars cofounder) — depression, OCD
- **Andrea Perdomo** (Techstars alumna) — burnout while growing her company
- **Matthew Helt** (former Techstars senior implementation consultant) — anxiety, panic attacks, OCD while building business and family

**One claim worth keeping:** *"Highly driven and successful people often suffer from mental health problems too."* Entrepreneurial success does **not** protect against mental health issues.

**Crisis resources:** Page directs to immediate help if experiencing addiction, depression, suicidal thoughts, or crisis.
