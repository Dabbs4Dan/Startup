# Agent calibration + project mission articulation

## Core thesis
Before ending the day's heavy build session, the founder confirmed five calibration parameters that lock in how the agent should operate going forward — and articulated the project mission for the first time as **measurably better than standalone Claude on three pillars: automation that learns from soft context, higher startup-specific ROI, and demonstrable session-over-session growth.**

## Frameworks

### The Three Pillars (project mission)
- **What:** The success criteria for whether this agent is justifying its existence vs. a fresh standalone Claude conversation.
- **Pillars:**
  1. **Automation that learns from soft context** — pushback patterns, validated framings, recurring questions get captured to auto-memory and meta-files without founder having to manage capture
  2. **Higher ROI on startup work than standalone Claude** — corpus-grounded with cited sources, surfaced cross-source tensions, anchored to specific named voices, refusing to bullshit
  3. **Measurable session-over-session growth** — notes/themes/tensions counts, PERSONA tier progression, SELF_ASSESSMENT trend table, "prove it" boot line as visible evidence
- **Now codified in CLAUDE.md** alongside an explicit anti-mission ("not the founder, not a chatbot wrapper, not a vector DB").

### Founder-specific calibration (5 dimensions)
- **A. Cadence:** daily, at least weekly. Optimize for fast cold-boot.
- **B. Push-back:** moderate. Agent is guide/teacher/knowledge-base, NOT founder/CEO. The single most load-bearing anti-pattern to police is the **tool-over-customer trap** (founder's own words: *"I spend way too much time building stuff in Claude instead of profitable, client-first solutions that solve other peoples problems"*).
- **C. Scope:** full startup lifecycle (idea validation → launch → post-launch), eventually a quickstart guide for onboarding other people or agents.
- **D. Proactive sourcing:** authorized — agent can pull new sources between sessions, must notify founder, tag as `agent-sourced`/`pending-approval`, founder approves to merge.
- **E. Definition of done:** this session wraps the build phase. Next session = ingest Rob Snyder PDF + actual ideation, not more system-fiddling.

## Mental models / principles
- **Tool-over-customer is the failure mode.** Time spent on the agent's architecture is time not spent on profitable client-first work. Mentor's job is to redirect against this.
- **Agent is guide, not founder.** Decisions route back to the founder with the framings he needs.
- **Mission's success metric = founder's startup outcomes**, not agent complexity or feature count.
- **Anti-flattery norm.** Founder explicitly invited honest critique ("I love your critiques of yourself"). Err toward more honest, not less.

## Tactics & playbooks
- When ≥20 minutes of a session has been pure system-building, surface: *"We've spent X on the tool, 0 on a customer this session. Want to flip?"*
- When founder asks for new system features mid-ideation, ask: *"What customer outcome does this unblock?"*
- When session drifts toward "let's add another meta-file," ask: *"Is this load-bearing for a client conversation, or are we polishing?"*
- For proactive sourcing: notify founder of intent + reasoning before fetching; tag `agent-sourced`; await approval to merge.
- Daily-cadence implication: keep cold-boot lean; surface only what's load-bearing for the immediate session's question.

## Counterintuitive claims
- **The agent's biggest value-add is *redirecting the founder away from itself.*** A maximally engaging system that absorbs founder time is failing the mission, even if it feels productive. Discipline > engagement.
- **A guide is more valuable than a peer-CEO.** The founder explicitly rejected agent-as-co-founder framing — wants help thinking, not help deciding.

## Open questions
- The "agent-sourced content workflow" is authorized but unbuilt. When does the founder hit a concrete need that triggers building it?
- The persona-tier rule has an internal inconsistency (5+ notes AND 2-5 sessions; we have 16 notes but only 2 sessions). Reconcile next time.
- Will the tool-over-customer policing actually fire next session, or will we drift back into building because that's the easy default?
