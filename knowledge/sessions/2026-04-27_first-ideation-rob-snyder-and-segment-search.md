**The founder committed to Rob Snyder's sell-to-learn methodology** as the primary engine (with a paid-ads-vs-cold-outbound variant under negotiation), **but has not yet committed to a problem space or Melissa**. Domain pivoted from "B2B SaaS sales-org tooling at mid-market+" (founder's day job) to "SMB segment, TBD" (more approachable buyers). 12. **Rob Snyder YouTube video ingested** (separate note in `knowledge/youtube/`).

## Frameworks reaffirmed / introduced

- **Rob Snyder's 6-part case study** (Project / Context / Options / Results / How / What) — the unit of pre-PMF work.
- **The Asana model for demand** — *"which project on her board does my product fit into, and when does she prioritize it?"*
- **Mine → Interview lightly → Sell to learn** — the integrated demand-discovery sequence, drafted in `TENSIONS.md`.
- **Tiny-scale dual-channel testing** — paid ads + cold DMs in parallel, $200–500 ad cap, comparing case-study debugging quality.

## Tensions surfaced

- **3-pole demand-discovery tension** (Empathy interviews / Sell to learn / Mine signals) — see `TENSIONS.md`.
- **Channel-before-case-study trap** — picking how-to-fill-the-pipeline before the case study is debugged risks debugging against the wrong audience.
- **"Approachable buyer" vs "founder pain"** — founder's most-personal pain (slower-growth-SaaS sales orgs) is the worst-buyer segment. Real strategic trade-off, no resolution yet.

## Counterintuitive claims earned this session

- **"Build a tool to find problems" is the second-order tool-over-customer anti-pattern.** First-order = fiddle with the agent. Second-order = build a problem-discovery system instead of going to find one.
- **Inbound from paid ads ≠ founder magic.** Different signal — more tire-kickers, less "the message only YOU could send to only THEM" forcing function. Both useful; not interchangeable.
- **"SMB tar pit" is a worldview, not a fact.** Often told by enterprise-flavored sources. Indie hackers + several YC giants disagree.
- **Pulling back from "the segment I know" without a replacement is a pivot from *who* to *how*** — and the *who* is the unfinished work.
